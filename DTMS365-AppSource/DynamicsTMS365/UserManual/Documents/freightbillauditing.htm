Freight Bill Auditing

Freight bill auditing is a two-step process. First, approving fees that need approval and then running the audit process in the AP Freight Bill Audit Approval page. However, following your fee approval, the system will automatically trigger the audit process, if invoices pass audit it will automatically clear, thus saving users time. If the audit fails, these transactions will show up in M status and need to be fixed.

The Fee Approval page (Under Load Planning) allows users to review fees entered into the portal by carriers or via EDI and require approval for auditing purposes. Fee tolerances can be assigned to each fee and approval automation can be configured. See Carrier Page for Fee Tolerances set up.

Assuming freight bills have been entered by carriers via the portal or EDI, here are the steps to process and audit freight bills.

Freight bills will come in on a daily or random basis from carriers. It is important to create a standard process as to how often you will tend to transactions that need approval and managing the exceptions. Freight bills will not be sent to the ERP system unless they have successfully passed the audit routine in TMS (AA status). All transactions must end up in AA status (Approved) in order to be sent to the ERP accounting system.
